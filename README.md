<h2>Research on Digital Account Deactivation and Deletion</h2><br>

I researched whether major digital platforms have thought about what happens to user accounts after long-term inactivity or death. I focused on the most widely used apps, Google, Facebook, Instagram, Telegram. since these are platforms where people frequently share posts, chats, photos, and memories. However, my idea is not limited to these; it should be implemented in all applications that involve user interaction.

<h3>Google ‚Äì Inactive Account Manager</h3><br>

<h4>Features:</h4><br>

<li>Users set an inactivity period (3, 6, 12, or 18 months).</li>

<li>After this period, Google will notify trusted contacts (via email or phone).</li>

<li>Users can decide whether to share their data with trusted contacts or automatically delete their account.</li><br>

<h4>How to Enable:</h4>

<li>Go to Google Account > Data & Privacy > More Options > Make a plan for your digital legacy.</li>

<li>Set the inactivity period.</li>

<li>Add trusted contacts and choose what data they can access.</li>

<li>Decide whether your account should be deleted after inactivity.</li><br>

Pros: Full user control, flexible timeline, trusted contacts notified.
Cons: Complicated setup, not widely known, relies on users planning ahead.<br>

<h3>Instagram ‚Äì Memorialization</h3><br>

<h3>Why Companies Keep Inactive or Deceased Accounts</h3><br>

<h4>Most companies have little incentive to remove inactive or deceased accounts. Some reasons include:</h4><br>

üîπ 1. User Count = Business Value
Social media companies report ‚Äúactive users‚Äù to investors. The higher the number, the more valuable they look. Deleting inactive or deceased accounts would reduce these numbers and hurt business value.

üîπ 2. Data = Asset
Even if someone is deceased, their data (posts, likes, networks) is valuable. Companies use it for AI training, marketing analysis, and behavioral statistics.

üîπ 3. Digital Presence = Engagement
Friends and family often interact with memorialized accounts, which still generates engagement and traffic for the platform.

üîπ 4. Legal Loophole
In many countries, there are no strong laws about digital inheritance. This allows companies to keep accounts indefinitely unless family members request deletion.<br><br>
<h2>Smart Deactivation & Deletion System(my idea)</h2><br>

In today‚Äôs digital world, managing one‚Äôs online presence has become just as important as managing real-life responsibilities. <h3>The uniqueness of this idea lies in its dual-choice flexibility:</h3>

<li>Deactivation/Deletion After Inactivity ‚Äì the account will only deactivate or delete if the user remains inactive for the chosen duration.</li>

<li>Deactivation/Deletion Even If Active ‚Äì the account will deactivate or delete at the scheduled time, regardless of whether the user is active. This can be useful for people who want a digital detox or have already decided to step away from the platform permanently.</li>

<li>To make the system user-friendly, it also includes a warning and notification mechanism. Users would receive reminder emails or in-app messages before the scheduled date, giving them a chance to cancel or adjust the timer if they change their mind.</li>
